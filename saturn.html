SATURN

Saturn, the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the solar system, has a diameter of about 116,460 kilometers, making it a massive gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. Its mass is 95 times that of Earth, and it has such low density that it could float in water if there were a body large enough to contain it. Saturn is most famous for its stunning ring system, the largest and most complex in the solar system. These rings are made of ice and rock particles, ranging from dust grains to boulders. They span over 280,000 kilometers but are only about 1 kilometer thick. The Great White Spot is a recurring storm system that occurs about every 30 years. Saturn has at least 83 known moons, with Titan being the largest. Titan has a thick nitrogen-rich atmosphere and liquid methane lakes, while moons like Enceladus show water-ice geysers, suggesting subsurface oceans. Saturn's strong magnetic field extends far beyond the planet, and its rapid rotation (a day on Saturn lasts about 10.7 hours) gives it a noticeable equatorial bulge. NASA's Cassini mission explored Saturn for over 13 years, providing invaluable data.
