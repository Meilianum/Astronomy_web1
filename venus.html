VENUS

Venus, the second planet from the Sun, is often called Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size, composition, and proximity to the Sun. With a diameter of about 12,104 kilometers, Venus is just slightly smaller than Earth. However, the conditions on Venus are vastly different. Venus has a rocky, metal core surrounded by a thick atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid. This dense atmosphere creates a powerful greenhouse effect, trapping heat and making Venus the hottest planet in the solar system, with surface temperatures around 465°C (869°F)—hot enough to melt lead. The surface pressure on Venus is about 90 times that of Earth, equivalent to the pressure found 900 meters underwater on Earth. Thick clouds obscure the surface, but radar mapping has revealed vast plains, mountains, and large volcanic structures. Venus has a slow rotation, taking about 243 Earth days to complete one full rotation, and it rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, causing the Sun to rise in the west and set in the east. A day on Venus is longer than its year, which takes about 225 Earth days to orbit the Sun. Venus has no moons and is sometimes visible from Earth, earning it the names "Morning Star" or "Evening Star." Space missions like NASA's Parker Solar Probe and ESA's Akatsuki continue to study this mysterious planet.
